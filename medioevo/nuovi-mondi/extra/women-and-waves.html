Women and waves

Until the Western cultural takeover of Hawaii late in the 18th century and throughout the 19th century threatened to wipeout surfing, women surfed alongside men since the beginning of surfing in Polynesia. When surfing was reborn in Hawaii in the early 20th century, it was largely men that surfed. That was until a TV series in the 1950s and 60s called Gidget inspired a generation of female surfers in California. The culture, fashion, and women's professional surfing scenes have grown exponentially ever since. We're proud to present the following features related to women's surfing, female surf photographers, and surf artists.
